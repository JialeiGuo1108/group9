The Evolution of Transportation Networks

The Grand Canal, with a history spanning over two thousand years, has borne witness to the transformations of Chinese history. With the rise of modern transportation networks, its role has shifted from a transportation route to a carrier of cultural heritage. After being inscribed on the World Heritage List in 2014, cities along the canal have been striving to strike a balance between preservation and development, transforming the ancient waterway into a source of urban vitality.

The evolution from ancient water transportation to modern three-dimensional transport networks vividly illustrates the revolutionary transformation of China's transportation system—an intricate web of railways and expressways has replaced the once-dominant canals and post roads, marking a qualitative leap in both transport efficiency and coverage.

In the future, the Grand Canal will continue to serve as a cultural link, bearing witness to the ongoing development of modern transportation networks while ensuring the enduring vitality of this historic waterway of civilization.
